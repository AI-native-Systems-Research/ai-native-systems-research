# Analytics & Privacy

This page explains what data this site collects when you read a post, why, and what choices you have.

## What we collect and why

We use **Google Analytics 4** to count how many times each post is read. We do not collect anything for advertising, profiling, or cross-site tracking.

## What gets sent to Google Analytics

When you load a page on this site, the following are sent to Google:

- The page URL **without** query parameters or fragments (e.g., `/blog/2026/04/21/post-name/`)
- The page title
- The URL of the page that referred you here (if any)
- Your IP address &mdash; used briefly to derive your country, then truncated and discarded by Google before storage
- Your User-Agent string (browser type and operating system)
- Your screen resolution, viewport size, and color depth
- Your browser language and timezone offset
- Your country, region, and city, derived from your IP address before truncation

## What we do not collect

- **No cookies.** No `_ga`, `_gid`, or any other tracking cookies are set.
- **No persistent identifier.** Nothing is stored on your device that links your visits across sessions.
- **No demographics, interests, age, or gender.** Google Signals is disabled.
- **No advertising data.** Ad-personalization features are disabled and ad-related data is redacted.
- **No outbound link clicks, file downloads, scrolling, video, search, or form events.** Enhanced Measurement is disabled at the property level.

## How to opt out

You can prevent Google Analytics from running on this site at any time by:

- Using a content blocker such as **uBlock Origin** or **Privacy Badger**
- Enabling strict tracking-prevention mode in your browser (e.g., **Firefox Enhanced Tracking Protection** set to Strict, **Safari Intelligent Tracking Prevention**, or **Brave Shields**) — these block the analytics script at the network level
- Installing the [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)

We do not gate any content on this &mdash; opting out has no effect on what you can read.

**Important practical note:** we do not store any identifier that links visits to a specific person.
